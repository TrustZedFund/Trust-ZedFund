Why Trust ZedFund Exists

Many people want to save, learn, or support businesses but lack structure, trust, or financial clarity. Trust ZedFund exists to solve this gap by providing a transparent system where people can observe, learn, coordinate, and participate voluntarily.

Membership Types

1. Learner / Observer Membership

Designed for individuals who are starting alone, cautious, or simply want to understand how community finance works before committing money.

  • Access to financial education and practical insights
  • Visibility into how savings circles and pooled funds operate
  • No obligation to contribute money
  • No investment risk

2. Community Saver Membership

For groups such as families, friends, churches, cooperatives, or clubs that want to pool funds responsibly using clear rules and accountability.

  • Group-defined contribution rules
  • Transparent tracking and reporting
  • Funds remain under community control
  • Trust ZedFund provides structure, not decisions

3. Contributor / Venture Support Membership

For individuals or groups who want to support small businesses or community ventures with clear understanding of risk.

  • Access to vetted opportunities
  • Clear documentation and reporting
  • No guaranteed returns
  • Value depends on real economic performance

How Pooled Funds Work

Pooled funds belong to members or communities. Accounts are purpose-specific and governed by agreed rules. Trust ZedFund does not secretly use, trade, or lend funds.

Value only increases when real activities succeed. There are no artificial returns or promises.
